Overview

Out here, your phone can go from “fine” to “no service” in minutes. That’s normal— but it makes it harder for others to know your plan, your boundaries, and when to worry.

RidgeRelay is a concept for a lightweight outdoor safety system built around planning. You set your intent, timing, and check-in rhythm before you go. If check-ins stop, the system escalates in predictable steps—starting with your chosen contacts and only moving toward emergency support when it’s truly needed.

Safety-first by design: clear expectations reduce panic, false alarms, and wasted search time.

What RidgeRelay prioritizes

  • Planning over posting
  • Clarity over complexity
  • Trust through predictable steps
  • Offline-friendly mindset (concept)

How it works

A simple 4-step flow—built to feel calm under stress.

  1. 1

    Plan

    Declare your intent (where you’re going, when you start, when you expect to return). Add one emergency contact who should be notified first.

  2. 2

    Guardrails

    Set reasonable boundaries: route, timing, and a “latest acceptable return.” The goal is shared expectations—not perfect tracking.

  3. 3

    Check-ins

    Choose a cadence (e.g., every 30–60 minutes). If you miss a check-in, the system starts with gentle nudges and context for your contact.

  4. 4

    Escalation

    If time thresholds pass, RidgeRelay escalates in steps—contact, then secondary actions, then emergency support as needed. No surprises.

About

RidgeRelay is a concept prototype built as a school project.

This site demonstrates a calm, planning-first UX for low/no-cell recreation. It’s intentionally static (GitHub Pages) and uses open-source tools (Leaflet + OpenStreetMap). The goal is to explore how clear intent, guardrails, and check-in rhythms can reduce confusion when something goes wrong.

In a full product, offline maps, device capabilities, and an emergency escalation workflow would be carefully tested and designed with safety stakeholders. This prototype focuses on the user experience and information flow.

Prototype scope

  • Single-page UX concept
  • Demo planner UI
  • Leaflet map with local GeoJSON
  • No accounts, no feed, no tracking
